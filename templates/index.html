About SciComm

Empowering Young Innovators

SciComm University Competition is designed to inspire students to embrace science communication, promote innovation, and share groundbreaking ideas. This event encourages participants to present scientific concepts in engaging and understandable ways, bridging the gap between science and the public.

Our Vision

We aim to create a platform where future scientists and communicators converge, share knowledge, and build a community that supports innovative ideas that can make a lasting impact on the world.

Registration Instructions

1

Gather Your Team & School Representative

Form your team and confirm a representative from your school who will assist in the registration process and act as the primary contact.

2

Provide Team Information

Prepare all necessary details for your team. Here's what you'll need:

  • Team Name: A unique name that represents your team.
  • School: The name of the school you are representing.
  • Coach Name: Full name of the coach who will guide the team.
  • Number of Members: How many members are on your team.
  • Name & Age: List all team members' names along with their ages.
  • Chosen Topic: The topic that your team will work on for the competition.
3

Submit & Confirm Registration

Submit your team's registration form, and make sure to check your email for a confirmation link to complete the registration process.

Guidelines

Here are the guidelines to follow throughout the event:

  • Be respectful to others. Collaboration and courtesy are key.
  • Follow the submission requirements. Ensure your work meets all criteria.
  • Ensure your work is original. Creativity is encouraged, plagiarism is not.

Elimination Round

The elimination round will consist of:

  • Initial submission review. Your entries will be assessed for content quality.
  • Judging based on creativity. The creativity and uniqueness of your work will be evaluated.
  • Announcement of finalists. The best entries will proceed to the final round.

Final Round

The final round will be held on:

  • Live presentation to judges. Share your work and defend your ideas.
  • Q&A session with the panel. Engage in a thoughtful discussion about your project.
  • Award ceremony and feedback. Winners are announced, and constructive feedback is provided.

Criteria for Judging

Each project will be evaluated on the following criteria to ensure a fair and balanced assessment.
Criteria Description Weight
Innovation The originality and creativity of the solution presented. 25%
Feasibility How realistic and practical the implementation is. 20%
Impact The potential positive effect on the environment and society. 25%
Design & Usability The aesthetics, ease of use, and user experience of the solution. 15%
Presentation The clarity, structure, and delivery of the presentation. 15%

Note: The percentages represent the weight each criterion holds in the overall evaluation process.
