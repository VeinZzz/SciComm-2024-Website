Mechanics for the Science Communication (SciComm) Competition

1

Eligibility

The Competition is Open to Senior High School Students from Across the Country. The Partcipants may compete in teams up to three members.

2

Article Selection

Participants must select a scientific or techinical article related to one of the following fields:

  • Data Science
  • Information Technology
  • Civil Engineering
  • Industrial Engineering (Supply Chains/Logistics, Decision Sciences and Sustainability Analytics)

The chosen article must be published in a reputable source such as a peer-reviewed journal. For a list of journals, click here . For a sample list of articles, click here .

3

Identify Need/Problem

Participants must identify a specific stakeholder (e.g., a company, government agency, non-profit organization, or community). They must analyze the stakeholder‚Äôs business needs or problems relevant to the article‚Äôs topic.

4

Presentation Requirements

Participants will prepare a presentation addressing the following:

  • A clear summary of the selected article, including its objectives, methodology, and key findings.
  • The identified stakeholder and the specific business need or problem they face.
  • How the study or article provides insights or solutions to address the business need or problem.
  • The potential business impact or value of applying the article‚Äôs insights in real-world settings.

Presentations should use visuals (e.g., slides, infographics) to enhance clarity and engagement.

5

Submission

Participants must submt a digital copy of their presentation and the article used.

6

Competition Format

  • Preliminary Round - Submissions will be evaluated by a panel of judges based on clarity, relevance, and alignment with the competition's goals
  • Final Round - Shirtlisted participants will deliver a live presentation to the judging panel, followed by a brief Q&A Session
